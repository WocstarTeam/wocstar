    Wocstar Academy

    The Wocstar Academy isn’t just a program—it’s a deep-dive into the mechanics that move you from founder to master of the raise by providing the definitive, insider playbook for securing the capital you deserve. Likewise, allies learn to move from occasional supporters to savvy, successful investors.

    Empowering Founders and Investors

    Wocstar Academy’s program gives entrepreneurs direct insights from active funders, expert guidance to sharpen their storytelling, and tailored strategies for investor pitches, grants, and loan proposals. This nationally recognized experience includes curated workshops, one-on-one mentorship with industry leaders, and a supportive community of peers. The support continues after the program through networking opportunities and curated introductions that help accelerate each founder’s journey.

    Curriculum Blueprint

    The playbook that turns ambition into investable momentum

    Built for founders raising with precision and allies investing with confidence. Practical, high-caliber training designed for real outcomes.

    Founder Curriculum

    Insider intelligence for the raise

    • Strategic Capitalization: Identify the right capital for your stage, from seed to scale.
    • Valuation & Terms: Master the math and mechanics of negotiating fair, growth-oriented deals.
    • Campaign Execution: Build and run a professional fundraising engine that closes rounds.
    • Investor Cultivation: Move beyond the pitch to build lasting, high-value investor alliances.

    Investor Curriculum

    Mastering the asset class

    • Asset Class Mastery: Deep-dive into venture capital fundamentals and early-stage equity mechanics.
    • The Diligence Framework: Evaluate tech innovation and spot the edge others miss.
    • Portfolio Strategy: Understand risk management, diversification, and long-term return design.
    • Inclusive Sourcing: Use a female-forward lens to find and fuel high-potential, under-tapped talent.

    How We Learn: Community & Flexibility

    Collaborative Power: Group coursework with founders and investors, powered by collective intelligence and shared networks.

    Wocstar Online Academy (Coming Soon): Self-paced modules for leaders who want world-class training on their own schedule.

    Gayle Jennings-O'Byrne is the CEO of Wocstar Capital and co-founder of the Wocstar Fund, an early-stage venture capital fund that invests in a new generation of innovative and diverse tech entrepreneurs. Recognized by the New York Times as one of "10 Women Changing the Landscape of Leadership," she has been named one of Forbes "50 Over 50" investors and a "Top Black Venture Capitalist" (Business Insider) and a "Top 10 Woman of Influence in Venture Capital" by Venture Capital Journal. Her track record of identifying and empowering visionary founders has resulted in successful fund exits that have returned investor capital. Gayle also leads Wocstar Academy, a global program that trains CEOs for capital readiness, and oversees a major grant on behalf of Pivotal Ventures and other foundations to address systemic underinvestment in female fund managers.

    Gayle has over 30+ years of Wall Street, technology, philanthropy, training, and professional speaking experience. She is a former mergers and acquisitions banker at JPMorgan. She led the capital/planning effort for the Cinema High School, the 1st public high school of its kind. She began her career at Sun Microsystems.

    She is a graduate of the Wharton School of Business, the University of Michigan, and an attendee of the National University of Singapore and the City of London Polytechnic.

    She is the former President of The Nantucket Project Academy and a former board member of Tri Delta Foundation Board, Women.NYC, NYC Economic Development Corporation, and board member of BE.NYC (Black Entrepreneurs), NYC Department of Small Business Services. Gayle is a 2022 recipient of the U.S. Presidential Lifetime Achievement Award, the 2021 Tri-Delta Woman of Achievement Award. Gayle's media experience includes M&A of film studios and assets, Associate Producer of "Thoughts of a Colored Man" a Broadway play (October 2021) and investor in "For Colored Girls Who Have Considered Suicide / When the Rainbow Is Enuf" (April 2022) which was nominated for seven Tony Awards as well as investor in several films and media projects.

    Wocstar Media Fellows

    2024 - 2025 Media Fellow

    Nicki Micheaux

    Actor, Writer, and Director

    Nicki Micheaux was named Wocstar Media Fellow in 2024. Nicki exemplifies the creative entrepreneur who uses storytelling to take audiences on transformative journeys. Armed with over 30 years of industry experience and the skills honed in the directing program, Nicki completed her first feature film, Summer of Violence, in 2024.

    Summer of Violence highlights a Black woman’s poignant coming-of-age story with depth and resonance. The film captivated audiences and premiered in theaters nationwide. In recent years, Nicki has also shifted her creative vision toward writing and directing to expand the stories she brings to screen.

    In addition to her creative pursuits, Nicki developed and launched Elevate Creative Talents, a course designed for actors, writers, directors, and producers to move projects from script to screen. She is also an ambassador for Moms of Black Boys United, reflecting her commitment to both art and advocacy.

    2026 Media Fellow

    Randy Donaldson

    Tony Nominated Producer

    Producer of critically acclaimed Broadway and Off-Broadway shows such as The Scottsboro Boys, American Son, The Pee Wee Herman Show, One Funny Mother, Triassic Parq, and Falling For Eve.

    Randy is the Executive Producer of Marian, a new musical inspired by the life and legacy of Marian Anderson. He is also producing the upcoming production of Mr. Rickey Calls a Meeting. With a deep commitment to building an inclusive theater landscape, Randy focuses on stories that broaden representation, create opportunity, and invite audiences into powerful cultural conversations.

    Randy joins the Wocstar Media Fellows Program in 2026, where he will develop new work and expand pathways for diverse voices across the theater industry.
